Advantage

With both health and dental services under one roof, it’s easier to coordinate care, especially if a health issue overlaps with dental needs. For example, a child with a chronic health condition may require specific considerations in their dental care. Having access to both professionals within the same clinic supports a more holistic approach to a child’s well-being.

Procedures

Preventive care, promoting healthy habits from an early age. Pediatricians work on monitoring growth, vaccinations, and overall wellness, while dental professionals provide cleanings, sealants, and fluoride treatments to prevent cavities and promote oral hygiene.

Frequently Asked Questions

The Best Queries

How often should I visit the dentist?

It’s recommended to see a dentist every six months for routine cleanings and checkups. Regular visits can help identify potential issues early, which is essential for maintaining oral health.

What vaccines does my child need?

Vaccination schedules vary by age and country, but common vaccines include those for measles, mumps, rubella, polio, and flu. Your pediatrician can provide a schedule tailored to your child.

How can I boost my child’s immune system?

FHealthy habits like a balanced diet, adequate sleep, regular physical activity, and proper hygiene can support your child’s immune health. Encourage handwashing, especially before meals, and ensure they get enough sleep each night.

How can I manage my dental anxiety

Let your dentist know about your anxiety so they can provide support. Techniques like deep breathing, listening to music, and visiting an office specializing in gentle dental care can make visits easier.

At what age should my child start using fluoride toothpaste?

For children under three, use a small smear of fluoride toothpaste. After age three, a pea-sized amount is recommended. Fluoride helps prevent cavities and strengthens enamel, but consult your dentist for personalized advice.

When should I take my child to the doctor for a fever

For infants under three months, any fever warrants a doctor’s visit. For older children, consult a pediatrician if a fever is above 102°F (39°C), lasts more than a few days, or if your child shows other concerning symptoms.
